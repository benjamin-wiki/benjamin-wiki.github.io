Problem Solving

13 April 2023

1. Blocked on a simple problem

I was struggling to implement a function that would allow me to filter an array of objects based on a specific property value. I tried using for loops and if statements, but my solution became increasingly convoluted and difficult to read. Frustration grew as multiple attempts failed to produce a clean and efficient solution. Lessons Learned: Eventually, I discovered the JavaScript array method filter(), which allowed me to easily achieve the desired result in a more concise and readable manner. I learned the importance of researching and exploring new techniques when faced with roadblocks in coding.


2. Elegantly solved a problem

I needed to create a function that would take a list of numbers and return the sum of all even numbers. I opted for an approach, using the filter() and reduce() array methods, that I had just learnt about. As I worked through the solution, I felt a sense of accomplishment and satisfaction with the the code. Lessons Learned: The experience taught me the value of exploring different methods and approaches to solving problems.

4. Reluctance to ask for help

I dont usually feel reluctant to ask someone for help. I've learnt that you actually look a fool when you pretend to know something. Most of the time people want enjoy helping others, it makes them feel good which makes me feel good! And I enjoy asking for help because I like to learn! :D


Problem-solving can be both challenging and rewarding. Learning to embrace various techniques, including asking for help, is crucial for success. As I continue to develop my skills, I will strive to maintain an open mind, learn from my experiences, and reflect on my problem-solving approaches to become a more effective and confident programmer.
