Foundation Reflections

29 April 2023

What are three new things I have learnt about myself and my ego due to the core learning?

* I found out that I truly enjoy reaching a flow state during coding sessions, which keeps me engaged and focused.
* I realised that I struggle with fully listening and absorbing information, so I need to work on improving my listening skills.
* I discovered that I find satisfaction in refactoring and formatting code, making it more readable and easier to understand.


What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

* Values guide my actions and decisions, shaping my learning journey and the way I approach programming challenges.
* Empathy allows me to connect with others, understand different perspectives, and collaborate effectively, which is crucial in programming teams.
* Self-awareness helps me recognise my strengths, weaknesses, and learning style, enabling me to tailor my approach to learning and programming for better results.


What has surprised me the most about the core learning?

I was most surprised by the strong emphasis on personal growth and well-being in addition to technical programming skills. I also found the focus on collaboration and pair programming to be enjoyable and beneficial for understanding different problem-solving approaches.


What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I found it challenging to maintain focus, deal with feelings of being overwhelmed by new information, and work with a self-critical mindset. Additionally, starting exercises was difficult for me, and managing my well-being throughout the learning process was a challenge.


Why do I think a programming school is spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

The focus on core learning in a web development Bootcamp helps students like me develop a strong foundation in personal growth, empathy, and self-awareness, which are essential for success in the programming world. These skills not only improve learning outcomes but also foster better collaboration and problem-solving in professional environments.


Does the time I spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should I have just used that time to practice programming instead?

No, the time spent on core learning doesn't feel like a waste of time. While practicing programming is essential, developing a strong foundation in personal growth, mindfulness, and self-awareness enhances my overall learning experience and long-term success. By balancing technical skills with personal growth, I'm better prepared to tackle programming challenges and collaborate effectively with others in my future career.


With the insights gained from my reflections, I now have a better understanding of my strengths, areas for improvement, and the importance of personal growth in my programming journey. This will help me continue to develop both as a programmer and an individual.
